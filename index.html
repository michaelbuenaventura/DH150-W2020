DataNet: A Better Research Tool

DH 150 UX Design, Michael Buenaventura

Introduction. Have you ever created and distributed a survey? Even with the most advanced tools, this can take a good deal of time. Getting insights from humans in general is a long, drawn-out process. My goal is to cut out mundane tasks to streamline this process.

Design Statement. I want to contribute to the human-subjects research community with this project. Much of research on humans requires data collection via surveys and forms. Creating, distributing, and analyzing these instruments take up a significant amount of time in the research process. Making this process more efficient through app-based distrubition can save both researchers and participants valuable time and energy. More time can then be devoted to thorough experimental design and analyses.

Conclusion. While I put a ton of effort into creating my prototype, it is far from perfect. It can benefit a heuristic evaluation and testing from other users. While ideas for the app seemed so clear at first, prototype creation brought me back to reality. Creating a streamlined tool is not intuitive and requires lots of trial and error.

I gained a wealth of knowledge and experience from this project. UX design is an even longer process than human-subjects research. UX design involves gaining insights from humans initially via usability testing and contextual inquiry. From these insights, designers must then create something new. I appreciate all the hard work of Professor Cho, giving us a thorough perspective on UX design.
